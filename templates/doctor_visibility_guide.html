Grow Your Practice

Practical tips to rank higher, connect with patients, and handle reviews professionally

Why Your Photo Matters

Patients form an impression within seconds. A professional photo creates an immediate sense of trust and approachability.

  • Profiles with photos get 3x more views than those without
  • Patients feel more comfortable booking with someone they can "see"
  • A warm, professional expression signals empathy and competence
  • Avoid: blurry images, group photos, or photos in casual settings
Pro tip: Use a headshot with good lighting, wearing professional attire. A genuine smile works better than a serious expression.

Complete Your Profile

Every field you fill increases your chances of being found by the right patients.

  • Bio: Write 2-3 sentences about your approach to patient care
  • Experience: Patients trust doctors with clear experience listed
  • Specializations: Be specific about what conditions you treat best
  • Workplace: Helps local patients find you more easily
How ranking works: Profiles with more complete information appear higher in search results. Verified profiles get an additional boost.

Understanding Patient Psychology

Patients searching online are often anxious, uncertain, or in discomfort. Understanding this helps you connect better.

  • They want reassurance: Clear credentials and a professional presence reduce their anxiety
  • They compare: Patients typically view 3-5 profiles before deciding
  • Reviews matter deeply: Even one detailed positive review can be the deciding factor
  • Response time signals care: Doctors who respond to reviews appear more engaged
Key insight: Patients aren't just looking for qualifications — they're looking for someone who will listen and care.

Handling Reviews Like a Professional

Reviews — both positive and negative — are opportunities to demonstrate your professionalism.

For Positive Reviews:
  • Thank the patient genuinely (without being overly promotional)
  • Keep responses brief and warm
  • Never reveal any medical details in your response
For Negative Reviews:
  • Don't take it personally — the patient is expressing their experience, not attacking you
  • Wait 24 hours before responding to avoid emotional reactions
  • Acknowledge their experience without being defensive
  • Offer to discuss offline if appropriate: "Please contact our office so we can address your concerns"
  • Never argue, blame, or reveal patient information
Remember: Future patients read how you respond to criticism. A calm, professional response to a negative review can actually increase trust.

Why Patients Leave Negative Reviews

Understanding the root cause helps you respond with empathy rather than frustration.

  • Unmet expectations: They expected one thing and experienced another (often a communication gap)
  • Feeling unheard: The most common complaint isn't about medical skill — it's about feeling rushed or dismissed
  • Pain and frustration: Patients in discomfort may express themselves harshly
  • Wait times: Long waits without communication breed resentment
  • Staff interactions: Sometimes the review is about reception staff, not you
Growth mindset: Every negative review is feedback. Even unfair ones reveal what patients value. Use them to improve communication, not to question your competence.
Important
  • You cannot edit or delete patient reviews
  • Never include patient names or medical details in review responses
  • Responding to reviews is available only for verified doctors
  • Fake or solicited reviews violate platform policies
